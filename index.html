2008 Housing Crisis

Introduction

The 2008 housing crisis was a global financial crisis triggered by the collapse of the housing bubble in the United States. It led to a severe economic downturn and widespread mortgage defaults, affecting millions of people.

Causes

Several factors contributed to the crisis, including risky mortgage lending practices, financial products tied to home loans, and the deregulation of financial institutions. Many homeowners found themselves with unaffordable mortgages and were forced into foreclosure.

Impact

The crisis had devastating effects on both homeowners and the global economy. Millions lost their homes, unemployment soared, and financial markets collapsed, leading to the worst recession since the Great Depression.
